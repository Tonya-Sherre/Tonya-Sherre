# FAQ 💻✨

## Why are you transitioning into IT?
Technology was actually my original passion. I originally attended The Art Institute in Nashville for Game Design before the school closed. After spending years in healthcare and earning my degree in Surgical Technology, I realized I wanted to return to the technology field long-term.

I became especially interested in cybersecurity because of its complexity, troubleshooting, and problem-solving aspects. I enjoy the idea of analyzing systems, understanding how things connect, and solving issues like a puzzle. After being introduced to tools like CrowdStrike while shadowing with the IT department at my hospital, I realized cybersecurity and infrastructure work aligned closely with the same fast-paced problem-solving skills I developed working in trauma care.

## Why cybersecurity?
I’m interested in networking, system security, and eventually working in a SOC environment helping protect systems and infrastructure.

## What are you currently studying?
I recently earned my CompTIA A+ certification and am currently studying for Network+ while attending WGU.

## What projects are you working on?
Currently building beginner PowerShell automation projects and learning more about troubleshooting, networking, and scripting fundamentals.

## How does your healthcare background help?
Working in a Level 1 trauma center taught me how to stay calm, adapt quickly, and solve problems under pressure. As a trauma scrub tech, I often had to anticipate the needs of surgeons during emergent situations and quickly create solutions with the resources available in the moment. That environment strengthened my communication, troubleshooting, teamwork, and attention to detail skills.
